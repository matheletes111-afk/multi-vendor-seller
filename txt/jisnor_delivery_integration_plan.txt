================================================================================
   INTEGRATION PLAN: JISNOR4U DELIVERY PARTNERSHIP
   Merging with Product & Food Delivery Systems (Multi-Vendor Marketplace)
================================================================================

Currently, all delivery logistics, costs, and statuses are handled manually by the individual sellers (Product Sellers & Restaurant/Food Sellers) under a "Self-Delivery" model. 

This document outlines a simple, progressive plan to integrate Jisnor4U as our official tech-enabled, standard delivery partner for both product and food orders.

---

1. ARCHITECTURAL OVERVIEW & INTEGRATION OPTIONS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Jisnor4U offers two ways to calculate delivery fees and handle fulfillment:

Option A: Redirect-Based Integration (Frictionless / App-to-App)
  - At checkout, if the customer chooses "Jisnor4U Delivery", they are redirected to the Jisnor4U Web/App during the checkout flow.
  - The customer enters their address inside the Jisnor4U UI, which calculates the distance-based delivery fee.
  - The customer confirms, pays (or returns to our platform to pay), and is redirected back to us with the tracking details.
  - *Pros:* Simple UI work on our end; address autocomplete and routing are outsourced to Jisnor4U.
  - *Cons:* Disrupted customer experience (switching between platforms); harder to handle combined orders.

Option B: API-Ready Server-to-Server Integration (Recommended for Premium Experience)
  - Our platform queries the Jisnor4U API in the background.
  - We fetch the real-time, distance-based delivery price by sending the shop's geolocation/address and the customer's delivery address.
  - We show the delivery price directly on our native checkout pages (no redirect).
  - Once the customer places the order, we automatically book the delivery dispatch via Jisnor4U's API.
  - We pull real-time tracking status and rider information to display in the customer's order history.


2. STEP-BY-STEP IMPLEMENTATION PLAN
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

STEP 1: DATABASE & SCHEMA UPDATES
We need to update our database models (Prisma) to store delivery fees, delivery options, and integration identifiers.
- Order / FoodOrder Table Updates:
  * deliveryType: Enum ('SELLER_SELF_DELIVERY', 'JISNOR4U') - allows a transition period or seller choice.
  * deliveryFee: Decimal/Float - to store the computed delivery charge (now no longer hardcoded to FREE or 0).
  * deliveryTrackingId: String (optional) - to store Jisnor4U's unique tracking identifier.
  * deliveryStatus: Enum ('PENDING', 'ASSIGNED', 'PICKED_UP', 'DELIVERED', 'CANCELLED').
  * deliveryRiderInfo: JSON (optional) - stores rider name, phone, and coordinates for live tracking.
- Seller / Restaurant Profile Updates:
  * longitude & latitude (or exact address coordinates) - necessary to calculate the distance-based pricing from the merchant's physical shop.

STEP 2: RETAIL PRODUCT CHECKOUT INTEGRATION
- Modify `src/app/checkout/checkout-client.tsx` and `/api/customer/checkout/place-order/route.ts`:
  * Add a "Select Delivery Method" section with:
    1. Standard Seller Delivery (seller handles it)
    2. Jisnor4U Express (calculated in real time)
  * When Jisnor4U is selected, make an API call to a new helper endpoint `/api/delivery/calculate-fee` passing the cart items (to group by merchant locations) and the customer's selected address.
  * Dynamically add the computed `deliveryFee` to the Grand Total before placing the order.

STEP 3: FOOD/RESTAURANT CHECKOUT INTEGRATION
- Modify `src/app/foods/checkout/page.tsx` and `/api/customer/foods/orders/route.ts`:
  * Replace the static "FREE" delivery charge label.
  * Query the delivery fee calculator. Since food delivery is typically single-restaurant per order, this is straightforward:
    * Fetch coordinates of the restaurant (RestaurantSeller).
    * Fetch coordinates of the selected customer address.
    * Call the pricing helper to get the fee.
  * Display the calculated delivery fee dynamically and include it in the checkout total.

STEP 4: AUTOMATING DISPATCH (BACKEND TRIGGERS)
- When a payment is successful or COD is confirmed:
  * If `deliveryType === 'JISNOR4U'`, automatically make a POST request to Jisnor4U's booking API:
    ```json
    {
      "pickup_address": "Seller Store Address / Coordinates",
      "pickup_phone": "Seller Phone",
      "delivery_address": "Customer Address / Coordinates",
      "delivery_phone": "Customer Phone",
      "order_id": "ORD-12345",
      "callback_url": "https://our-marketplace.com/api/webhooks/jisnor4u"
    }
    ```
  * Save the returned tracking ID and initial status to the database.

STEP 5: REAL-TIME TRACKING & WEBHOOK HANDLER
- Create `/api/webhooks/jisnor4u/route.ts` to receive status updates from Jisnor4U:
  * When a rider is assigned → Update order `deliveryStatus` and save rider details.
  * When rider picks up the order → Automatically transition OrderItem status to `SHIPPED`.
  * When rider marks delivery complete → Automatically transition OrderItem status to `DELIVERED` (and trigger any related wallet credits/payout releases).
- Customer Portal:
  * Update the customer order tracking pages (`/customer/orders` and `/foods/orders`) to show a live tracking card (e.g., Map with rider coordinates, rider name, rider phone) if the delivery is managed by Jisnor4U.

STEP 6: SELLER PANEL UPDATES (PRODUCT & RESTAURANT)
- Currently, sellers manually update status from PENDING -> CONFIRMED -> PROCESSING -> SHIPPED -> DELIVERED.
- With Jisnor4U:
  * The seller only needs to accept the order and mark it "READY FOR PICKUP".
  * Marking it "READY FOR PICKUP" triggers the Jisnor4U dispatch call.
  * Subsequent statuses (SHIPPED, DELIVERED) are automated via the Jisnor4U webhook, reducing manual overhead for sellers.

3. REVENUE & PAYMENT SPLITTING MODEL
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
- As per the proposal, the customer pays the delivery fee at checkout via our marketplace.
- The marketplace must settle this delivery fee with Jisnor4U.
- **Sellers/Restaurants Ledger:** 
  * Update the ledger calculations (e.g., `src/lib/restaurant-ledger.ts`) to deduct the `deliveryFee` from the seller's payout if the marketplace collects and forwards it, or ensure it is accounted for in admin commissions.


4. CRITICAL BUSINESS & TECHNICAL CONSIDERATIONS FOR THE CLIENT
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Before finalizing the integration, the following operational logic needs to be agreed upon with the client:

A. Multi-Vendor Cart Splitting (Multi-pickup orders)
  * Problem: If a customer checks out with items from Seller A (Wilkinson Road) and Seller B (Charlotte Street) in the same order, a single rider cannot easily do both pickups unless charged extra.
  * Solution options:
    1. Calculate two separate delivery fees based on pickup locations and charge the customer the sum.
    2. Split the checkout into separate orders per seller, each with its own delivery fee (standard industry practice).

B. Fallback & Serviceability Bounds
  * Problem: If a customer's address is outside Jisnor4U's 10 designated zones, or if the API is down.
  * Solution: Detect unserviceable zones at address input and fall back automatically to "Seller Self-Delivery" or show "Not Serviceable" to the customer.

C. Cancellation and Refund Workflow
  * Problem: If a customer cancels their order.
  * Solution:
    * If cancelled BEFORE dispatch -> Issue a full refund. Send an API call to Jisnor4U to cancel rider request.
    * If cancelled AFTER dispatch -> Charge a partial fee (as per Jisnor4U terms). Deduct this fee from the customer's refund amount.

D. Staging / Sandbox Environment
  * We will request sandbox API credentials and mock endpoints from Jisnor4U to test rider dispatch, status webhooks, and fee calculation before going live.
